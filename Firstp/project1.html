Data Vizualization - Audio Features of spotify's top 100 songs


The Data & Process

As a competitive swimmer, music helps to relive stress before the swim meets or competitions. When traveling,preparing for a swim meet, or even when reading long tedious research papers,Spotify comes in hand. I’ve always wondered why certain songs are ideal for those specific moments. For example, what is that one thing about a Bon Jovi’s song “It’s My Life” which makes you feel energetic or pumped up , after spending  some time with Professor Internet , I discovered there are audio features to every song which decides it genre., which also decides listener’s mood, while learning more about it I came across spotify’s raw data from 2017 and I decide to do a data visualization of it, I considered this would make a challenging and interesting data visualization.

Next, I tried to import the created features fields and track URLs to link with each other at webpage object on Tableau, but it didn’t play the track, then I checked the URLs with personal Spotify playlist and observed that last one character was missing from all the track IDs. Hence, I exported the playlist to excel files and corrected all 100+ IDs.

Usability Study

· Three Users: 2 males, 1 female
·  Age range: 19-27
· Education:In college or graduated from college
· Occupations:financial rep., student, and accountant
· Favorite artists: Kendrick Lamar, Billie Eilish, Jay-Z
· Genres:Hip hop, alternative
· Paid monthly subscriptions: 2 (1 Apple Music, 1 Spotify)
· Main device used: 2 smart phones and 1 car


Usability Analysis

Task 1: Find one of the two artists that had the most songs
in the playlist, and play a song of choice.
(test parameters: circular artists visualization &amp;artist dropdown)
Task 2: Find each feature of the song played in task one and point out the measurements
(test parameters:feature visualizations understanding)
Task 3: Find three provided songs and find out their liveness and acoustics, and decide which one of them are best fit to relax to.
(test parameters: navigation and how they process (compare) the data
Task 4: Play a song by your favorite artist in the playlist and explain which feature was most important to you.
(test parameters: understand feature importance)

Observations

1.Users often utilized sound over the visualizations when making decisions on song features.
2.Users found the artist dropdown menu easy and quick to use.
3.It was not obvious to all users that selecting/playing a song would result in the highlighting of the song’s features on each visualization.

Results

Experience that is visual and direct
